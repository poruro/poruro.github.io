Do you want to grow up feeling sorry for not choosing the right path/career in your life? Or if you are an adult, do you want to be on your deathbed regretting things you could have done? No, right! We all want to live a happy, fulfilled, successful and desired life. And this can only happen when we know the purpose of our life.


On completing my formal education, I opted for a Jewelry designing diploma course and also completed a diamond grading diploma.

Now if I try to analyze why I joined those courses, then the answer I got is maybe because they were different from what others were doing. Or maybe there was no one to guide me in the right direction. Or maybe I didn’t know how to choose a career out of passion.

I tried different businesses like I was into jewelry, candle making, trousseau packaging, running an institute for kids, etc.

I made good money doing them. But I was never happy with what I was doing. I never felt that peace or satisfaction doing what I was doing.

Many people asked me, “Why do you need to work? You have such a lovely family, why don’t you spend your life taking care of them?” Yes, I love spending time taking care of my family but that is not all I want. But seriously, I didn’t know what I wanted from life. I was fighting with myself to give meaning to my life. Somewhere, I had lost interest in life and then came the game changer of my life when I started questioning myself. What is my purpose in life? Now I can proudly say I have found my purpose in life following the techniques that I will be teaching you in this book.


I see many people are confused between ‘THE GOAL AND PURPOSE OF LIFE.’

Your life purpose is the ultimate end you have in the sight of where you want to end up. A goal is a benchmark through which you hope to achieve your life purpose. Think of your life purpose as an overall strategy and your goals as a way to keep you on your path so that you arrive where you ultimately want to go.

For example, say your life purpose is to give all happiness and comforts to your family and contribute to the well-being of society. So, fulfilling your purpose in life could be setting goals like earning extra money by improving some skills or doing a part-time job, etc. so you can contribute to your purpose in life.

OR

One of your broader life purposes might be to empower others to take better care of the environment. A goal that will help you honor this purpose could be as ambitious as organizing a neighborhood campaign to clean up the local park, or as simple as teaching your child to turn the light off when they leave the room.
